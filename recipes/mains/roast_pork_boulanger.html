Roast Pork Boulanger

Ingredients

  • 3-1/2 lb. center pork loin
  • 1 clove garlic, slivered
  • 4-1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried thyme leaves
  • 1/4 teaspoon plus 1/8 teaspoon pepper
  • 3 lb. medium potatoes, peeled and thinly sliced
  • 1 cup chopped onion
  • 1 tablespoon chopped parsley
  • 1/2 cup canned condensed chicken broth, undiluted (I create broth using powder and water)
  • 1/4 cup butter, melted (I use less - have used as little as 2 tablespoons)
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • chopped parsley

Directions

  1. 1. Preheat oven to 425°F. Wipe pork with damp paper towels. Trim off excess fat (I never do this).

  2. 2. Rub outside of pork with cut garlic; then insert garlic slivers where possible in crevices. Combine 1-1/2 teaspoons salt, the thyme, and 1/4 teaspoon pepper; rub mixture all over surface. Place pork, fat side up, in large shallow roasting pan.

  3. 3. Roast 45 minutes. Remove pork from oven; discard fat in roasting pan.

  4. 4. Gently toss potatoes with onion, 1 tablespoon chopped parsley, and the remaining salt and pepper. Arrange around roast. Heat chicken broth to boiling; pour over potatoes. Brush potatoes with melted butter. Sprinkle with paprika.

  5. 5. Reduce oven to 400°F. Roast pork 1 hour longer, or until meat thermometer registers 170°F and potatoes are fork-tender and nicely browned. Sprinkle potatoes with chopped parsley. Serve pork and potatoes from roasting pan. Makes 8 servings.
